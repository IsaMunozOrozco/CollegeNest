Research

The Research Process

By definition research is "the systematic investigation into and study of materials and sources in order to establish facts and reach new conclusions" (Oxford Languages). In other words, it is a rigiourous procedure for how we understand the world, whether from a micro (e.g., atomic, microbial, etc.) or macro (e.g., whole-body, societal) level. The National Institute of Health has a great article and inforgraphic, but we'll try and explain it furthur here.

The research procedure can generally be explained in a few steps but you must know that the process itself involves multiple steps and is an iterative process. It is not a linear path. The exact steps also differ based on the lens in which you approach your topic, whether you are using primary or secondary data, among other factors. For instance, two scientist can approach the same problem or phenomenon with different approaches. Science in Motion summarizes it well (see video below).




Below you will find resources for each "step." Please know that research is a team effort, so reach out to your teacher/professor, research supervisor, principal investigator, and/or school librarian for help.
